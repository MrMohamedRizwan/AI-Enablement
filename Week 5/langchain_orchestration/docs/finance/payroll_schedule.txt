Payroll Schedule and Information - Employee Guide

OVERVIEW
========
This document provides information about payroll processing, schedules, and related procedures.

PAYROLL SCHEDULE
================

Pay Frequency: Bi-weekly (every two weeks)
Pay Periods: 26 pay periods per year
Payday: Every other Friday

2026 Payroll Calendar:
- Pay Period 1: Jan 1-14, Payday: Jan 17
- Pay Period 2: Jan 15-28, Payday: Jan 31
- Pay Period 3: Jan 29-Feb 11, Payday: Feb 14
- Pay Period 4: Feb 12-25, Payday: Feb 28
- [Full calendar available on HR portal]

Holiday Pay Schedule:
- If payday falls on a holiday, pay is processed the business day before
- Holiday pay included in regular payroll
- Vacation and sick time processed with regular pay

PAYROLL PROCESSING TIMELINE
============================

Pay Period End (Sunday):
- Time sheets due by 11:59 PM
- Expense reports must be submitted
- Any payroll changes finalized

Monday-Tuesday:
- Payroll team processes timesheets
- Managers approve overtime and adjustments
- Benefits and deductions calculated

Wednesday:
- Final payroll calculations completed
- Direct deposit files sent to bank
- Pay stubs generated and distributed

Thursday:
- Payroll verification and quality checks
- Exception reports reviewed
- Last-minute corrections processed

Friday (Payday):
- Direct deposits available by 9 AM
- Pay stubs accessible online
- Payroll support available for questions

DIRECT DEPOSIT
===============

Setup Requirements:
- Bank account information (routing and account numbers)
- Voided check or bank letter
- Direct deposit authorization form
- Processing time: 1-2 pay periods for new setups

Multiple Accounts:
- Split deposits between up to 3 accounts
- Specify dollar amounts or percentages
- Savings and checking accounts supported
- Changes take effect next pay period

Backup Payment:
- Paper checks available if direct deposit fails
- Pickup required from HR office
- Reissue fee may apply for lost checks

PAY STUB INFORMATION
====================

Access Pay Stubs:

Pay Stub Sections:

OVERTIME POLICY
===============

Overtime Eligibility:
- Non-exempt employees only
- Hours worked over 40 in a work week
- Time-and-a-half rate (1.5x regular hourly rate)
- Manager pre-approval required

Overtime Calculation:
- Based on actual hours worked
- Vacation and sick time don't count toward overtime
- Holiday pay doesn't count toward overtime
- Calculated weekly, not daily

Double Time:
- Hours worked over 12 in a single day (where applicable)
- Holiday work (certain holidays only)
- Seventh consecutive day worked

TIME TRACKING
=============

Hourly Employees:
- Must clock in/out using time tracking system
- Lunch breaks: 30 minutes minimum (unpaid)
- Break times: 15 minutes (paid)
- Missed punches require manager approval

Salaried Employees:
- Expected to work standard business hours
- Flexible schedule with manager approval
- Overtime not typically paid
- Time off tracked for PTO purposes

Time Tracking System:
- Online portal: https://time.company.com
- Mobile app available
- Biometric or badge scanning
- Manager approval for corrections

PAYROLL CHANGES
================

Salary/Wage Changes:
- Effective date specified by HR
- Prorated for partial pay periods
- Retroactive adjustments processed next payroll
- Written notification provided

Tax Withholding Changes:
- Submit new W-4 form to HR
- Changes effective next pay period
- Online form submission available
- Annual review recommended

Direct Deposit Changes:
- Submit new authorization form
- Allow 1-2 pay periods for processing
- Verify account information carefully
- Old account information kept on file

YEAR-END PROCESSING
===================

W-2 Forms:
- Available by January 31st
- Electronic delivery preferred
- Paper copies available upon request
- Corrections processed as needed

Tax Documents:
- W-2: Wage and tax statement
- 1095-C: Health insurance coverage (if applicable)
- 1099: Contract payments (if applicable)
- State tax documents as required

Final Paychecks:
- Processed on regular payroll schedule
- Unused PTO paid out (per policy)
- Final expense reimbursements included
- COBRA information provided

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS
==========================

Q: When will I receive my first paycheck?
A: Typically 2-3 weeks after start date, depending on pay period

Q: Can I change my direct deposit information?
A: Yes, submit form to HR with 1-2 pay periods processing time

Q: What if my paycheck is wrong?
A: Contact payroll immediately; corrections made next pay period

Q: How do I access old pay stubs?
A: Log into payroll portal; 7 years of history available

Q: When are W-2s available?
A: By January 31st each year, electronically and by mail

Q: Can I get an advance on my paycheck?
A: Generally not available; speak with HR about hardship options

IMPORTANT REMINDERS
===================

- Keep direct deposit information current
- Review pay stubs for accuracy
- Update tax withholdings annually
- Report payroll errors immediately
- Maintain confidentiality of pay information
