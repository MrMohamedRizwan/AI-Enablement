Budget Reports and Financial Statements - Access Guide

OVERVIEW
========
This guide explains how to access and understand company budget reports and financial statements.

AVAILABLE REPORTS
=================

Monthly Budget Reports:
- Department budget vs. actual spending
- Variance analysis and explanations
- Forecast updates and projections
- Key performance indicators (KPIs)

Quarterly Financial Statements:
- Income statement (P&L)
- Balance sheet
- Cash flow statement
- Executive summary and highlights

Annual Reports:
- Comprehensive financial review
- Audit results and findings
- Strategic initiatives and investments
- Regulatory compliance reports

Department-Specific Reports:
- Individual department budgets
- Project-based financial tracking
- Cost center analysis
- Resource allocation reports

ACCESSING REPORTS
==================

Finance Portal:
- URL: https://finance.company.com
- Login with company credentials
- Navigate to "Reports" section
- Select desired report type and period

Report Categories:
- Executive Dashboard (C-level access)
- Department Reports (manager access)
- Project Reports (project manager access)
- Employee Reports (limited access)

Monthly Report Schedule:
- Reports available by 10th of following month
- Department summaries by 5th of month
- Executive dashboard updated weekly
- Real-time spending data available 24/7

UNDERSTANDING BUDGET REPORTS
============================

Key Metrics:
- Budget: Original allocated amount
- Actual: Money spent to date
- Variance: Difference between budget and actual
- Forecast: Projected spending for full period
- Remaining: Budget left to spend

Color Coding:
- Green: Under budget (favorable variance)
- Yellow: Within 5% of budget (caution)
- Red: Over budget (unfavorable variance)
- Blue: Information only (no variance threshold)

Common Report Sections:
1. Executive Summary
2. Revenue Analysis
3. Expense Breakdown
4. Variance Explanations
5. Forecast Updates
6. Action Items

BUDGET VARIANCE ANALYSIS
========================

Favorable Variances (Under Budget):
- Lower than expected costs
- Delayed project spending
- Efficiency improvements
- Market conditions

Unfavorable Variances (Over Budget):
- Unexpected expenses
- Scope changes
- Market price increases
- Emergency purchases

Required Actions:
- Variances >10% require explanation
- Variances >20% require corrective action plan
- Monthly variance reports due by 15th
- Budget revisions require approval

DEPARTMENT BUDGET ACCESS
========================

- Their department's budget and actuals

- Forecast scenarios

Employees Can View:

Restricted Information:

MONTHLY BUDGET PROCESS
======================

Week 1 (Month End):
- Departments close monthly books
- Expense reports finalized
- Accruals and adjustments made
- Preliminary numbers available

Week 2:
- Finance team reviews and validates data
- Variance analysis completed
- Department consultations held
- Draft reports prepared

Week 3:
- Final reports published
- Department briefings scheduled
- Executive presentations prepared
- Action plans developed

Week 4:
- Budget revisions processed
- Next month planning begins
- Quarterly projections updated
- Annual budget adjustments

BUDGET PLANNING CALENDAR
=========================

Annual Budget Cycle:

Quarterly Reviews:
